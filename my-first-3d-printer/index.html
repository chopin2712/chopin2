Happy new year! This is a new post about a wonderful gift I received for Christmas.

A kit of the Creality Ender 3 Pro.

This is my setup story of the printer.

Go to the store.

You can get this printer in many shops like Amazon or other but this printer come from IdeaTo3D. I am not related to this shop, this is only where I've bought it.

Assembly process.

Once you've got your package you need to build it, you have a guide that you can follow but you can also search on the web for other resources. Also, I didn't notice that there was a video on the SD card that in the package.

This is a video of the assembly of this machine:

I recommend you to use the guide to build it and only use the video when you are stuck. Also, I recommend you to take the assembly like a puzzle, this will probably take you few hours before finishing the building, even if the printer is "simple to build".

Calibration.

Then you would probably say that the printer is working but no, you also need to calibrate it.

This is a small guide to calibrate your 3d printer:

  1. Make sure the bed (the plate) can move without being blocked by other things.
  2. Use a calliper (or a level) to measure the top axis this need to be horizontal, so same height on both sides.
  3. Make sure the bed is horizontal.

For the last one, this is the most important and complex. You will notice lots of people who use a sheet of paper to calibrate their printer, but this is inefficient, slow, and hard. I recommend you to use live levelling as shown below.

  1. Download and extract the following thing on Thingiverse : https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2549082
  2. Import the corresponding file into Cura and maybe adjust it to make sure it can be printed (you can make it smaller, move it and change the down-face using the tools on the left)
  3. Slice it and save it into your SD card. This above steps will no longer need to be done.
  4. Print the file and when the filament is getting printed, check it with your fingers. If it is not solid enough, then make the bed higher using the knob under the bed. If it is too flat, then make the bed lower.
  5. To check it, restart the step 4 to make the final calibrations.

This whole calibration process should take you about 20 to 30 minutes or less. You can also do other techniques of live levelling, like using 5 squares on the bed and changing the settings while one of them is getting printed, but this take more time and this is harder. But this is probably more precise. This is a video about this last technique:

Your first print.

Then your printer should be OK so now, lets print!

  1. Create a solution of 50% Water and 50% Alcohol.
  2. Put your solution on the heat bed.
  3. Install "Cura" on your computer.
  4. Plug the SD card adapter with the SD card into your computer.
  5. Download and extract the "20 mm Calibration cube" here.
  6. Drag'n'drop the STL file inside the archive's file directory into Cura.
  7. On Cura select your 3D printer from the list, your filament, and your print quality (should be OK).
  8. Click on Slice and save to the SD card. Now you know how long the filament will be used and the approximated time to print.
  9. Eject the SD card and plug it to the printer.
  10. Go into the menu, select print from card then select your file.gcode.

Once the file is selected, the bed will heat to 60° then the extruder will heat until 200° then the first layout will be printed then the print will really start. The progression of your print is on the screen of the printer.

Then when it is finished you should get an exact 20 mm by 20 mm cube with X Y and Z on the sides.

To remove it use a soft tool to get it off, if it is still not working try pushing the smooth tool with a butter knife or something (like a hammer but more soft).

If your print stick to the plate, you can use the tool to force it, this will be easier with the solution on the plate. Also, If it is too hard make sure your bed is cold (less than 60°).

How to get other files to print.

You can get other STL files from internet, on websites like Thingiverse for example. Or you can design your owns using software like FreeCAD. I will write you another post about FreeCAD.

To print the files you just need to do the same thing as shown above with the SD card and let it run in the background when you're away. Just make sure the 3D printer is still calibrated and your cat is away.

You just need to sometimes check if the print goes on.

Hope this guide will help you in your 3D printing journey!
